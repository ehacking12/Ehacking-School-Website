Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small files of letters and numbers that we store on your browser or the hard drive of your computer if you agree. Cookies contain information that is transferred to your computer's hard drive.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the operation of our website. They enable you to move around the website and use its essential features, such as accessing the member login area. Without these cookies, services you have asked for cannot be provided.

  • Session management cookies
  • Authentication cookies
  • Security cookies

2.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies allow us to remember choices you make (such as your user name or language preference) and provide enhanced, more personalized features. These cookies also allow us to honor your preferences.

  • User preference cookies
  • Language selection cookies

2.3 Analytical/Performance Cookies

These cookies are used to collect information about traffic to our website and how visitors use our website. The data collected is aggregated and anonymous, meaning we cannot identify you personally through this data. These cookies help us understand which areas of our website are used most frequently and to optimize our website accordingly.

  • Usage analytics cookies
  • Performance monitoring cookies

2.4 Marketing/Promotional Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement and help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.

3. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Keeping you logged in to your account
  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Tracking your learning progress within courses
  • Analyzing how our website is used
  • Personalizing your experience on our website
  • Securing your account and transactions

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may allow third parties to place cookies on your device. These third parties may include analytics providers and advertising networks. We do not control how third parties use their cookies, and you should check their websites for details about their cookie policies.

5. Your Cookie Choices

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through your browser settings. You can:

  • See what cookies you have and delete them
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete cookies when you close your browser

How to Control Cookies:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies and other site permissions

6. Impact of Blocking Cookies

If you block cookies, certain features of our website may not function properly. You may not be able to:

  • Log in to your account
  • Access your course progress
  • Receive personalized content recommendations

7. Cookie Policy Updates

We may update this Cookie Policy at any time. When we do, we will modify the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. Your continued use of our website following the posting of revised Cookie Policy terms means that you accept and agree to the changes.
